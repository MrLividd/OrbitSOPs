Criminal Investigations
Department

Standard Operating Procedure for all officers attached to the Criminal Investigation Department of the Orbit Police. Intelligence-led, evidence-driven, professional at all times.

00 📖

Definitions

ℹ️
The following terms appear throughout this SOP. CID officers are expected to use them accurately in reports, briefings, and radio communications.
TermDefinition
CIDCriminal Investigation Department — responsible for serious and complex crime investigations.
CallsignUnique identifier assigned to each officer.
MDTMobile Data Terminal — in-game system used for records, intelligence, and reporting.

M 🎯

Mission Statement

🎯
The Criminal Investigation Department is committed to investigating serious and organised crime through intelligence-led policing, professional conduct, and evidential integrity. CID officers will operate with discretion and diligence to ensure successful prosecutions and maintain public confidence.
01🔍

Operations

1.1Conduct & Responsibilities▼

CID officers shall hold themselves to the highest professional standards. The integrity of any case rests on the conduct of those investigating it.

  • Act with professionalism, integrity, and impartiality at all times.
  • Prioritise evidence-based investigations over immediate enforcement.
  • Maintain strict confidentiality regarding ongoing investigations.
  • Use force only when necessary and proportionate.
  • Work collaboratively with uniformed units and specialist divisions.
⚠️
DISCLOSURE OF AN ONGOING INVESTIGATION TO ANY UNAUTHORISED PERSON IS A SERIOUS BREACH OF CONDUCT. This includes other officers not attached to the case, family, friends, or members of the public.
1.2Structure & Supervision▼

CID operates under a structured rank system. Every operation must be authorised and recorded through the appropriate supervisory rank.

DCI
Detective Chief Inspector
Unit Command. Strategic oversight of CID operations.
DI
Detective Inspector
Operational oversight. Authorises significant deployments.
DS
Detective Sergeant
Case supervision. Authorises undercover deployments.
DC
Detective Constable
Front-line investigations and case work.
FO
Forensic Officer
Probationary CID officer. Forensic and evidence focus.
ℹ️
All CID operations must be authorised and recorded through the appropriate supervisory rank. No officer operates independently of the chain — every case is owned, every action is logged.
1.3Callsign▼
  • Officers must display their callsign number when in overt operations.
  • Callsigns may be concealed during undercover or covert deployments.
1.4Issued Equipment▼

CID officers may be issued the following equipment, depending on role and deployment type:

  • TASER / Baton — for personal safety and use of force where required.
  • Radio communications device — primary tactical comms.
  • MDT access — for records, intelligence, and case file management.
  • Evidence collection kit — bags, markers, camera.
  • Covert recording equipment — when authorised by a DS or above.
  • Drone — requires drone certification before deployment.
⚠️
All issued equipment is the personal responsibility of the officer. Loss, damage, or misuse must be reported to a Detective Sergeant or above immediately.
1.5Dress Code▼

CID dress code varies by deployment. A more detailed view of dress code is available in the Uniform SOP. The four primary categories are summarised below.

🦺 1.5a — Overt Attire
  • Day-to-day duty attire.
  • Smart casual — suits, blouse, jeans, etc.
  • CID vest must be worn at all times outside the station.
👔 1.5b — Plain Clothes
  • Primary CID operating attire.
  • Clothing must suit the operational environment (realistic RP standard).
  • No visible police identifiers during covert or UC roles.
🕶️ 1.5c — Covert Clothes
  • Attire relating to the undercover profile.
  • Casual or formal as the cover identity requires.
  • Must be disguisable — no police markers visible at all.
🧫 1.5d — Forensic Uniform
  • White full-body forensic coveralls.
  • Protective face mask & disposable gloves.
  • Steel toe-capped boots.
  • Hair secured / tied up at all times.
1.6Operational Duties▼

CID officers are responsible for the investigation of serious and organised crime within the City of Orbit. Core duties include:

  • Investigating serious and organised crime.
  • Conducting PACE-compliant interviews where applicable in RP.
  • Surveillance and intelligence gathering.
  • Executing warrants and planned operations.
  • Building case files for prosecution.
02🚗

Vehicles

ℹ️
CID has access to three vehicle classes. The right vehicle for the right job — using a marked car for a surveillance task or a covert car for routine patrol both compromise operational effectiveness.
2.1Marked Vehicles▼
  • Primary CID transport for patrolling normal duties.
  • Used for support roles or joint operations with uniformed units.
  • Not recommended for surveillance or sensitive deployments — fully identifiable as police.
2.2Unmarked Vehicles▼
  • Secondary CID transport.
  • Used for surveillance and rapid response without drawing attention.
  • Retains police identifiers on closer inspection — not suitable for sustained covert work.
2.3Covert Vehicles▼
  • Fully civilian in appearance.
  • No emergency lighting or police identifiers.
  • Reserved for undercover or high-level surveillance operations.
⚠️
Covert vehicles must not be used outside of their intended operational purpose. Deploying a covert vehicle for routine duties compromises its value when it is genuinely needed.
03🕵️

Undercover Operations

⚠️
UNDERCOVER OPERATIONS MUST BE AUTHORISED BY A DETECTIVE SERGEANT OR ABOVE. No officer may deploy in a UC capacity without an MDT operation report filed and approval logged.
3.1UC Deployment Protocol▼

Undercover work is one of the most sensitive functions of CID. Identity, cover, and safety must be planned before any deployment commences.

  1. Operation must be authorised by a Detective Sergeant or above.
  2. Officers must file a detailed MDT report on the undercover operation before deployment.
  3. Establish and maintain a consistent cover identity — names, history, mannerisms, attire all aligned.
  4. No use of overt police equipment while undercover. This includes radios visible to the public, warrant cards, body armour, and standard issue items.
  5. Backup units should be available where practical — a covert support presence within reach.
  6. ANY COMPROMISE OF IDENTITY MUST BE REPORTED IMMEDIATELY. Extraction takes priority over the operation itself.
⚠️
Cover identities are not improvised — they are built. Officers operating without a properly documented UC profile risk both their own safety and the integrity of any evidence gathered.
04🧪

Evidence Handling

🧪
EVIDENTIAL CONTINUITY IS THE FOUNDATION OF EVERY PROSECUTION. A break in chain of custody, mislabelled item, or unrecorded handling can collapse a case at trial. Every exhibit, every handover, every action — logged.
4.1Evidence Collection▼
  • Evidence must be collected in accordance with RP realism standards.
  • Photograph evidence prior to collection wherever possible — original position is part of the evidence itself.
  • Avoid contamination of scenes — forensic uniform where required, gloves at minimum.
4.2Packaging & Storage▼

All evidence must be:

  • Bagged appropriately — correct evidence bag for the item type.
  • Labelled clearly using the evidence pack.
  • Logged into the MDT or evidence system once back at the station.
⚠️
Separate items to prevent cross-contamination. For example: blood samples must be packaged in one flat pack and the suspect's belongings in another. Never mix biological samples with general exhibits.
4.3Evidence Examination▼
  • Only trained officers may process or test evidence.
  • Results must be documented clearly and accurately — no shortcuts, no informal notes.
  • Maintain continuity of evidence at all times — every transfer, every handover, every signature.
4.4Documentation & Submission▼

All evidence must include the following details on submission:

FieldDetail
DescriptionClear description of the item — including make, model, colour, condition.
LocationWhere the item was recovered (room, position, surface).
Time & DateWhen the item was collected.
OfficerCollecting officer's name and callsign.

Reports must be:

  • Clear — no ambiguity, no jargon for its own sake.
  • Detailed — enough that another officer can pick up the file without questions.
  • Written professionally — court-ready from the moment of submission.
05🎓

Training & Accreditation

ℹ️
All CID specialist functions require formal certification before deployment. Operating outside your accreditation is a serious breach of conduct and may compromise prosecutions.
5.1Drone Operations▼
  • Requires certification before deployment.
  • Used for surveillance, search, and crime scene overview.
  • Drone footage forms part of the evidence file — handle as you would any other exhibit.
5.2Crime Scene Management▼
  • Scene preservation techniques.
  • Evidence identification and marking.
  • Contamination prevention — cordons, forensic suits, common approach paths.
5.3Evidence Handling▼
  • Proper collection and packaging.
  • Chain of custody procedures.
  • Court-ready documentation.
5.4MDT Usage▼
  • Recording crimes and intelligence.
  • Managing case files.
  • Conducting background checks.
⚠️
ALL MDT STATEMENTS MUST BE FINISHED BY THE TIME THE SUSPECT IS MAKING A PLEA. Any and all statements should be completed at the earliest opportunity. Failure to do so may result in a strike.
5.5Undercover Accreditation▼
  • Scenario-based training — realistic deployment simulations.
  • Maintaining cover identity under pressure.
  • Communication and emergency protocols — extraction signals, code words.
  • Building and managing undercover profiles.
